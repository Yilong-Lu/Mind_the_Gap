## Purpose and Procedure of the Experiment

The purpose of this experiment is to measure how human independently set different task goals. In this experiment, you will first complete some scales related to your mental status. Then, you will be asked to answer relevant questions based on a list of items within a given scenario.

## Risks

This study involves only computer-based tasks, so there are no inherent risks.  The task involved in the experiment does require some mental effort.
This study includes some questions related to mental health: about your emotions, your reactions in certain situations, and your level of agreement with certain thoughts or feelings. If you encounter any questions during the experiment that make you uncomfortable, you may stop the experiment at any time.

## Benefits

Aside from the monetary compensation, there are no direct benefits to you from participating in this study. However, we hope that this research will contribute to the scientific understanding of human intelligence, benefiting society as a whole.

## Confidentiality

Your online username may be linked to your responses. Other than basic demographic information and experiment-related data, we will not request any other personal identifying information from you, and we will handle your responses as confidentially as possible. However, we cannot guarantee the confidentiality of information transmitted over the internet. We will indefinitely retain the de-identified data collected in this experiment. Any data used in scientific publications will remain completely anonymous.

## Attention
To ensure the accuracy of your responses, the experiment includes attention-check questions. If you answer these incorrectly a certain number of times, the experiment will be terminated. Please answer carefully.
